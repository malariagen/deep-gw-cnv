Experiment 05 results summary:
- PM2_PM3: FNR=0.98 (367 FN, 7 TP) — near-total failure; HMM discarding real signal
  (FN p50 CRR=1.47)
- GCH1: FNR=0.29, PPV=0.95 — persistent; FN p50 CRR=1.18 ≈ 1.0 suggests weak
  latent signal, not just HMM
- CRT: FNR=0.04, PPV=1.00, MCC=0.98 — near-perfect; bin-count gate worked as predicted
- MDR1: FNR=0.11, PPV=1.00 — FPs eliminated; some FNs remain (FN p50 CRR=1.43)

My diagnosis:
The bin-count gate cleanly fixed CRT and MDR1 FPs. PM2_PM3 is the worst failure —
signal is clearly there (CRR p50=1.47) but Viterbi discards it. GCH1 is different:
FN p50 CRR=1.18 is close to 1.0, pointing upstream to the VAE representation rather
than the HMM. If the VAE latent space doesn't separate amplified from normal coverage,
the HMM has nothing to work with. PM2 amplification is extremely common in Pf9, so the
model may have learned to encode it as "normal." The evaluation guidance flags this
directly: high FNR can result from the model treating CNV-positive counts as the
default state.

Proposed experiment 06 (revised): Retrain VAE with higher beta (max_beta 1.0 → 4.0)
Changes from 05:
- max_beta: 1.0 → 4.0
  Stronger KL regularisation forces a more structured latent space. Instead of letting
  the model use all latent capacity freely, higher beta compresses variation into fewer
  effective dimensions — normal and amplified coverage should occupy more distinct regions,
  giving the HMM cleaner signal.
- warmup_epochs: 50 → 100
  Reconstruction needs time to stabilise before KL pressure becomes large. Longer warmup
  avoids the model collapsing the latent space before it has learned useful structure.
- hmm_self_transition held at 0.75 (exp 05 value) to isolate the VAE effect.

What I expect:
GCH1 FNR should improve — the weak-signal FNs are most likely to benefit from a more
structured latent space. PM2_PM3 is an HMM transition bottleneck; better VAE
representations may help at the margins but won't fully fix it (we can address that
with hmm_self_transition tuning in exp 07 once we see the VAE improvement). CRT and
MDR1 should stay stable.

What we could do instead:
1. Lower hmm_self_transition (0.75 → 0.65) — directly fixes PM2_PM3 without retraining;
   original exp 06 plan. Fast (~25 min), but doesn't address the upstream VAE issue.
2. Increase latent_dim (10 → 20) — more capacity; complementary to higher beta but
   less targeted at latent structure.

Run time estimate: Full VAE retrain (~3–4 hrs on Mac mini MPS) + HMM ~25 min + eval ~2 min.
