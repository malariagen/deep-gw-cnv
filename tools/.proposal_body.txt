Diagnosis
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The CRR BY CALL OUTCOME table is the key signal. Every gene's false
negatives had FN p50 well above 1.0 (1.53 for PM2_PM3, 1.35 for GCH1,
1.60 for MDR1, 1.67 for CRT). Per the evaluation guidance, FN p50 >> 1.0
means the reconstruction ratio signal IS elevated — the VAE is doing its
job — but the HMM is labelling those bins as CN=1 anyway. The HMM is
simply too sticky: with self_transition=0.99, it strongly resists leaving
its current state. The model also has no false positives, meaning we have
headroom to trade stickiness for recall. Additionally, PM2_PM3's
"pred_normal" CRR p50 = 0.90 (slightly below 1.0) suggests chromosome 14
has a mildly depressed baseline in many samples, causing the 80% CN=1
sanity check to quietly disqualify callable samples before a call is
even attempted.

Proposed experiment 02: HMM stickiness + CNV threshold tuning
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

No retraining required. Reuses the experiment 01 checkpoint. Only
HMM and CNV calling parameters change (~1–2 hours on Mac mini).

Changes from experiment 01:

  hmm_self_transition:    0.99 → 0.95  (less sticky; transitions to
                                         amplified states more readily)
  cnv_min_cn1_proportion: 0.80 → 0.70  (accommodate depressed chr14
                                         baseline in many samples)
  cnv_min_confidence:     0.70 → 0.60  (relaxed proportionally)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
What I expect
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FNR should drop meaningfully, especially for PM2_PM3 and GCH1. PPV may
dip slightly from 1.00 — lowering stickiness means more state transitions
and some risk of FP calls. If PPV drops badly (say below 0.80), the next
step would be to tighten cnv_min_confidence back up rather than reverting
the HMM change.
