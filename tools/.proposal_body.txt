Exp 19 results summary:
- PM2_PM3: FNR=0.01, PPV=0.93 — far exceeded predictions (predicted 0.10–0.25). Only 2 FNs remain.
- GCH1: FNR=0.07, PPV=0.91 — unchanged from exp 18. All 139 FNs have CRR just below the fallback gate (p50=1.24, p90=1.29).
- CRT and MDR1: near-perfect and stable (FNR≤0.03, PPV≥0.95).

My diagnosis:
Both PM2_PM3 failure modes from exp 18 are resolved. The fractional coverage check
fixed non-spanning segments, and the gate at 1.35 recovered gate-vetoed FNs. The
remaining bottleneck is GCH1. Because GCH1 spans only ~2 bins at 1000bp resolution,
it uses a simple CRR threshold (cnv_crr_amp_threshold=1.30) rather than the spanning/
fractional logic. All 139 FNs have CRR p50=1.24 and p90=1.29 — real signal that sits
just below 1.30. AF-E is the main driver (population FNR=0.32), especially pre-2010
samples. The signal is present; the threshold is the bottleneck.

Proposed experiment 20: Lower GCH1 fallback threshold to 1.25
Changes from exp 19:
- cnv_crr_amp_threshold: 1.30 → 1.25 (GCH1 fallback gate only).
  Recovers FNs with CRR in [1.25, 1.30] — roughly the top half of the FN distribution
  (p50=1.24 to p90=1.29).
- All other parameters unchanged (cnv_crr_gate_threshold=1.35 unaffected — it governs
  the long-gene fractional path, not GCH1).
- Reuses exp 19 checkpoint, reconstructions, and HMM segments via symlinks. No retraining.
- out_dir: 20_skip_st0.80_sin_partialspan_gate1.25.

What I expect:
GCH1 FNR should drop from 0.07 to ~0.03–0.04 as FNs with CRR ≥ 1.25 get promoted
from CN=1 to CN=2. pred_normal GCH1 p90=1.14, so there is a 0.11-unit gap before
the new threshold — very few true-normal samples should be affected. PPV predicted
to stay ≥ 0.89. PM2_PM3, CRT, and MDR1 are on a different calling path and will
not change.

What we could do instead:
1. Population-specific thresholds — AF-E (FNR=0.32) drives most of the GCH1 loss.
   A gene×population gate would concentrate the recovery without loosening globally.
2. self_transition=0.75 — longer HMM segments may aggregate weak GCH1 signal better.
   Still untested; try if exp 20 shows only partial recovery.

Run time estimate: ~30 minutes (CNV calling + evaluation only, no training or HMM).
